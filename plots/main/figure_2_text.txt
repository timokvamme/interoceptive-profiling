FIGURE 2. Minimal interoceptive subtyping network and item selection.
=====================================================================

Panel A: Item importance for predicting the mental health composite (TAS,
PHQ-9, GAD-7, STAI, SSS-8, PCS). Importance is a consensus rank from LASSO,
random forest, and network centrality.

Panel B: Saturation curve of cross-validated R-squared vs number of items.
The curve shows a strong performance gain with a small set of items and a
plateau near the 21-item solution.

Panel C: Network centrality vs predictive importance. Centrality is normalized
within each scale (IAS vs IATS) to avoid bias from different within-scale
correlation strengths.

Panel D: MDS network of interoceptive and mental health items. Distances are
based on 1 - |r|. Node size reflects absolute correlation with the mental
health composite; white rings mark the top 10 predictor items. Edges indicate
|r| > 0.30 and transparency scales with strength. N = 833.
